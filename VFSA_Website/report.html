Report an Incident

Your anonymous account helps build a research database that drives evidence-based policy for safer schools. All submissions are confidential and used solely for scientific analysis and policy research.

🔒
Your Privacy is Protected

This form is completely anonymous. We do not collect IP addresses, browser information, or any identifying data. No personal information is required. Your narrative becomes part of a phenomenological research database used by academics and policymakers to understand and prevent school violence.

Incident Report Form

All fields except the narrative are optional, but providing context helps researchers better analyze patterns and develop solutions. No personally identifiable information is required.

Please describe what happened in your own words. Include as much detail as you are comfortable sharing. Your narrative is the most valuable part of this report for researchers. Do not include names or other identifying details.

By submitting this report, you consent to your anonymous narrative being included in VFSA's research database for academic analysis, policy research, and educational advocacy. No personally identifiable information will be associated with your submission.

How Your Report Makes a Difference

Research

Phenomenological data from educator narratives provides rich qualitative evidence for academic research on school violence patterns.

Policy

Aggregated data informs legislators and policymakers about the real-world impact of school violence on educators and students.

Prevention

Understanding patterns helps schools and districts develop evidence-based prevention strategies and response protocols.
